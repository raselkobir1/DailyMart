Mini Departmental Store Management System - Complete Business Requirements Document BRD. 

NOTE:
This text file is intended as the master specification for building the application.

Architecture: Modular Monolith
Backend: .NET 8 Web API, ASP.NET Core, EF Core, Repository, Service, Unit of Work, Clean Architecture, JWT Authentication. 
Database: PostgreSQL
Frontend: Angular (Latest Stable version)
No CQRS, No MediatR.

========================================
PROJECT OVERVIEW
========================================
Shop Name: DailyMart
Build a production-ready Mini Departmental Store Management System that manages
the complete lifecycle of a super shop: suppliers, purchasing, inventory,
barcode-based POS sales, customer dues, supplier dues, expenses, reports,
profit/loss, dashboards and audit logs.

The primary goal of this application is to help a shop owner efficiently manage the entire business from purchasing products from suppliers to selling products to customers, maintaining inventory, tracking receivables/payables, calculating daily profit, and generating business reports.

This application should eliminate manual bookkeeping and provide real-time visibility into the overall business through a modern dashboard.

Business Scenario

A departmental shop owner purchases products from different suppliers.
The products are stored in inventory. Customers visit the shop and purchase products.
Some customers pay immediately. Some customers purchase on credit (due/baki).
Similarly, the shop owner may also purchase products from suppliers on credit.
The owner pays suppliers later.

The owner wants a complete software solution that manages all these operations and provides a real-time overview of the business.

========================================
BUSINESS RULES
========================================
Selling price cannot be lower than purchase price unless explicitly allowed.
- Barcode unique.
- Product code unique.
- No negative stock.
- Purchase increases stock.
- Sale decreases stock.
- Cash sale increases cash balance.
- Credit sale creates customer due.
- Credit purchase creates supplier due.
- Payment reduces supplier payable. 
- Partial payment updates both cash and due.
- All stock movement must be traceable.
- Customer due cannot become negative.
- Payment history must be preserved. 
- Supplier due must always match unpaid purchases.
- Every inventory transaction should have historuy.
